Part D. Explain the meaning of a p-value!

Explain in detail what it means specifically in a statistical sense for any result to be statistically significant at a particular -level. In other words, explain the meaning and use of p-values. You should research this question, and you should expect your answer to be at least a paragraph long. (6 pts) Answer: In the event that the null hypothesis is correct, a p-value indicates the probability of obtaining some test statistic as extreme as the one that was observed and essentially shows how likely it is to get the test results you did if the null hypothesis were true. It functions as a gauge of the evidence refuting the null hypothesis and we reject the null hypothesis when the p-value is less than or equal to the designated significance level (), indicating that the observed impact is statistically significant. A p-value of less than 0.05, for example, suggests that there is less than a 5% chance that the observed results are the result of random variation if is set at 0.05. As a result, we can figure out that the effect is statistically significant. Researchers can assess the strength of their findings and the possibility that the outcomes are not just the product of chance by using the p-value as a measure to verify their reuslts.
